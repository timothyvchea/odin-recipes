Mapo Tofu

What is Mapo Tofu?

Mapo Tofu is a spicy, savory dish from Sichuan, China. It is named Mapo tofu, as Mapo means pock-marked. It is said the the inventor of this dish was a pock-marked granny from Sichuan. It is a personal favorite of mine, and I consider it my staple dish.

It is famed for it's use of Sichuan peppercorns. The peppercorns are not very spicy, however they leave a numbing sensation on the tongue.

Ingredients

  • Tofu, preferably Soft
  • Sichuan peppercorns
  • Ground Pork
  • Green Spring Onions
  • Ginger
  • Garlic
  • Doubanjiang (Spicy Bean Paste)
  • Black Bean Sauce
  • Dark Soy Sauce
  • Chinese Cooking Wine
  • Chilli Oil
  • Chilli Powder
  • Corn Strach
  • Cooking Oil

Steps

  1. Chop the aromatics (Garlic, Ginger, White part of the Spring Onions) into small diced size pieces.
  2. Cut the tofu into small cubes.
  3. Put the tofu into a simmering not boiling pot of water. Take tofu out once it starts to float, it should take about 10 minutes.
  4. While tofu is bathing in the hot water, put wok onto a hot stove and pour in cooking oil.
  5. Once wok is smoking, put in ground pork.
  6. Break up the ground pork into smaller pieces
  7. Once ground pork is browned, add in the aromatics.
  8. Let aromatics cook for about a minute, then add in Doubanjiang and Black Bean Sauce.
  9. Stir fry for another minute.
  10. Pour in water until you can see the tops of the ground pork peeking out.
  11. Pour in Soy Sauce, Chinese Cooking Wine, Chilli Oil and Chilli Powder to your liking.
  12. Let sit for about 5 minutes.
  13. While the sauce is cooking, put some peppercorns into a hot unoiled skillet.
  14. Once peppercorns start to smoke, take them out and grind them.
  15. Cut the green parts of the Spring Onion to use as garnish.
  16. Pour the Mapo Tofu into another bowl.
  17. Garnish with green spring onion and the peppercorns.
  18. Done!
